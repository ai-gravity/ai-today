How Enterprises Are Building AI Agents in 2026

2026-05-21 · ~3 min · AI Tools

AI agents have undergone a remarkable transformation in recent years, evolving from experimental technologies to essential components of modern business infrastructure. As we move through 2026, organizations across industries are increasingly sophisticated in how they deploy these digital workers. To understand this shift, we partnered with research firm Material to survey over 500 technical leaders about their AI agent strategies and implementations.

The Evolution from Simple Automation to Complex Workflows

The data reveals a clear progression in how enterprises approach AI agents. Organizations are moving beyond basic task automation to implement sophisticated, multi-step processes that span teams and business functions.

Adoption Stage2025 Implementation2026 Plans
Multi-stage workflows57%81%
Cross-functional processes16%29%
Simple task automation89%95%

This evolution reflects growing confidence in AI capabilities and increasing sophistication in implementation approaches. Organizations are no longer content with agents that perform isolated tasks—they want collaborative digital workers that can navigate complex business processes.

When examining specific use cases, software development emerges as the leading area for AI agent adoption. The numbers are striking:

Nearly 90% of organizations now leverage AI to assist with development, with 86% deploying agents directly for production code. This widespread adoption has transformed development pipelines, enabling teams to move faster while maintaining quality standards.

Real-World Results

The theoretical benefits of AI agents are translating into measurable economic returns, with 80% of organizations reporting that their AI agent investments already deliver tangible value. Several examples illustrate this trend: Thomson Reuters has transformed legal research with Claude-powered CoCounsel, their AI legal platform. Lawyers who previously spent hours manually searching through documents can now access 150 years of case law and insights from 3,000 domain experts in minutes. In cybersecurity, eSentire has compressed expert threat analysis from five hours to just seven minutes, with AI-driven analysis aligning with their senior security experts 95% of the time. Healthcare technology company Doctolib rolled out Claude Code across their entire engineering team, replacing legacy testing infrastructure in hours instead of weeks and shipping features 40% faster. The retail sector has seen similar transformations, with L'Oréal achieving 99.9% accuracy on conversational analytics, enabling 44,000 monthly users to query data directly instead of waiting for custom dashboards.

As organizations scale their AI agent implementations, they face three primary challenges:

These challenges reflect the complexity of embedding AI agents into established business environments. Successfully addressing these issues requires strategic planning and appropriate infrastructure. The impact on workforce dynamics is significant. Nine in ten leaders report that agents are fundamentally changing how their teams work, with employees shifting their focus from routine execution to strategic activities, relationship building, and skill development.

Building for the Future

Organizations seeing the greatest success are treating AI agents as core infrastructure components rather than experiments. This approach requires purpose-built tools: models optimized for enterprise workflows, frameworks like the Agent SDK, and specialized tools like Claude Code that help teams move from prototype to production faster. As we look beyond 2026, the organizations building expertise in AI agent development and deployment today will capture disproportionate value as the technology matures and expands into research, customer service, financial planning, supply chain operations, and beyond. The question for leaders is no longer whether to adopt AI agents but how to scale them strategically to transform their operations and gain competitive advantage in an increasingly AI-driven business landscape.
