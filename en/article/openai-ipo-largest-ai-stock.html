OpenAI Files for IPO: The Largest AI Stock Offering in History Is Coming

2026-05-01 · ~1 min · Industry

Around May 22, 2026, the financial world was shaken by reports that OpenAI, the company behind ChatGPT, had filed a confidential S-1 document with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) in preparation for a public market listing. Trading is expected to begin by September 2026.

The Scale of the Offering

Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley are serving as lead underwriters. The target valuation ranges from $852 billion to $1 trillion, a figure that surpasses nearly every major tech IPO in history. If successful, it would become the largest AI-focused IPO ever recorded.

Revenue Numbers

OpenAI reported annualized revenue of approximately $11.6 billion in 2025, growing roughly 200% year-over-year. The main revenue streams come from ChatGPT Plus and Pro subscriptions, API usage fees for developers, and enterprise contracts with large organizations. This growth rate has captured significant attention from investors across the globe.

What Investors Should Know

Despite the impressive figures, there are reports that OpenAI's CFO internally warned that the company might not be fully ready for a public listing. This concern reflects the challenges that every AI company faces: extremely high costs for training models and running infrastructure, fierce competition from both established tech giants and nimble startups, and a regulatory landscape that remains unclear in many jurisdictions.

Impact on the AI Industry

OpenAI going public will not just be a story for investors. It will serve as a directional signal for the entire AI industry. If the market responds positively, it could draw significant investment capital toward other AI companies, accelerating innovation across the board. Conversely, a disappointing debut could make the market more cautious, potentially slowing the pace of AI funding. The broader implications extend beyond finance. A public OpenAI would face new pressures for transparency and quarterly performance, which could influence how the company approaches safety research, product development, and competitive strategy. Employees and customers alike may see shifts in company culture and priorities. Summary: The OpenAI IPO will be one of the most significant tech events of 2026. Whether you invest or not, following this story will help you understand the broader AI market landscape. A trillion-dollar valuation signals that the world views AI not as a passing trend but as foundational infrastructure for the new economy. The coming months will reveal whether that conviction holds up under public market scrutiny.
