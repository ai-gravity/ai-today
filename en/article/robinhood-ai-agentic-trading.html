Robinhood Launches AI Agent Trading — Crypto and Futures Coming Next

2026-05-28 · ~1 min · Industry

Robinhood, the popular US retail trading platform, has officially launched "Agentic Trading" — allowing customers to connect their own AI agents to trade stocks on their behalf without manual intervention.

How it works

Customers can set up their own AI agents to connect directly to Robinhood through the platform's purpose-built MCP (Model Context Protocol) server. The AI agent operates in a separate portfolio from the user's main account, with built-in safety controls and a real-time activity feed showing exactly what the agent is doing. The current beta supports equities only, but Robinhood confirmed that options, crypto, event contracts, and futures support are coming soon.

Why it matters

This isn't just another Robinhood feature — it signals the rise of "Agentic Finance," a fundamental shift in how trading works. Instead of humans making every buy/sell decision, AI agents analyze, decide, and execute trades autonomously using the MCP standard as the connection layer.

What competitors are doing

Coinbase launched Agentic Wallets for AI-driven crypto management Gemini allows AI bots to trade crypto directly on its exchange Robinhood CEO Vlad Tenev stated: "Our mission has always been to democratize finance for all, and now, that mission extends to AI agents."

But the numbers aren't pretty

Despite this ambitious launch, Robinhood's crypto revenue in Q1 2026 dropped nearly 50% year-over-year. However, the company recently completed a $200 million acquisition of crypto exchange Bitstamp, signaling long-term commitment to the space. Bottom line: The era of AI agents trading on your behalf has arrived. The real question is: how much trust are you willing to give an AI with your money?
